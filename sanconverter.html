Beat Up 8 — Hold & Roll Logic

(HWID Restricted)

Lane 7 (index 6) acts as a Hold/Roll marker in Arrow Vortex. When a Hold or Roll is active on lane 7, the converter applies special key-assignment rules to every note inside that Hold/Roll region.

Lane 7 Value Active Note Lane Key Assignment Jack Rule
2 (Hold Start) Lane 1 · 10000000 Random from {7, 4, 1} (Left) No key used twice in same Hold
2 (Hold Start) Lane 2 · 01000000 Random from {9, 6, 3} (Right) No key used twice in same Hold
2 (Hold Start) Any other lane · e.g. 00100000 Random from all keys {7,4,1,9,6,3} No key used twice in same Hold
4 (Roll Start) Lane 1 · 10000000 Random from {7, 4, 1} (Left) No direct repeat (no Jacks)
4 (Roll Start) Lane 2 · 01000000 Random from {9, 6, 3} (Right) No direct repeat (no Jacks)
4 (Roll Start) Any other lane · e.g. 00100000 Random from all keys {7,4,1,9,6,3} Jacks allowed (free repeat)
3 (Hold/Roll End) — Resets to standard BU8 logic —
  • Hold (2): Every note inside the hold gets a unique key — no key is repeated anywhere within the same hold region, regardless of side.
  • Roll (4): Lane 1 and Lane 2 notes avoid a direct repeat of the previous key (no Jacks). Notes on all other lanes are fully free and may Jack intentionally.
  • Side pinning: Lane 1 (10000000) is always pinned to the Left array {7,4,1}; Lane 2 (01000000) is always pinned to the Right array {9,6,3} — for both Hold and Roll.
  • Outside of any Hold/Roll region the standard BU8 random-key logic applies unchanged.
